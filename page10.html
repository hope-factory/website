Everyone who fundraises for a non-profit wants to raise more. Often they need to raise more. The conclusion – find new donors! This is the logical and seemingly universal answer. In fact, major donors and foundations frequently encourage non-profits to find “new” donors and offer grants to match their gifts. Non-profit leaders welcome these grants and invest the time and effort to find “new” donors.

Anyone raising money will tell you asking for money is “relational.” But the typical non-profit will see 55% of those who gave last year not give this year. The relational model is not working or better stated, it’s not practiced.

Many are unaware their back doors are open and donors are leaving in droves. For many non-profits it means failing to receive thousands, hundreds of thousands, or even millions of dollars in gifts annually! 

Donors are being left behind.

After more than 35 years in fundraising consulting,
Dr. Bill Hautt  is more convinced than ever we must
focus on retaining and recapturing donors.
